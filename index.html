As you move to your first enterprise pilots

As you move from pre-seed to your first enterprise pilots in construction and logistics, the question every sophisticated EHS buyer will ask is how these pieces actually orchestrate. That is the story we help product teams write.

The Eduba frame

Most of what any AI product does is not AI. In our experience about 60% of the work is traditional code and database operations (the pipeline that moves a frame from a site camera to a report), about 30% is rule-based logic (the hazard scoring you call predictive, which is mostly deterministic thresholds plus a small ML layer), and about 10% is genuine AI (the vision model that detects a missing helmet, and a language model that drafts a legible incident report from structured detections). Teams who do not separate these layers waste money running LLMs where Postgres would do the job faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees. A short advisory engagement gives you the map of your own stack.

The case

We ran this exact engagement for a product company whose CTO was trying to operationalize AI internally. Scope was a workshop, two weeks of advisory calls, and two deliverables: an Organizational Context Architecture and a Strategic Operations Framework. The output was a methodology that let the team pitch "how we build" with the same rigor as "what we build."

We published the Interpretable Context Methodology ("ICM: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture") in the ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems pipeline. It is the spine of how we think about stacks like yours.
